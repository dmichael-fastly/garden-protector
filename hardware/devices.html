How this page works

Every gadget in your garden shows up here once we spot it — cameras, sensors, and deterrents on your network. For each one: give it a name you'll recognise, double-check what it is, and press Add to garden.

Watcher or Deterrent?

This is the gadget's job in your garden.

Watcher — keeps an eye out and reports what it sees (cameras, motion sensors, microphones…).
Deterrent — does something to shoo critters away (a water sprayer, an alarm, a flashing light…).

What kind of gadget?

The specific type. We make a best guess from what we detected — change it if it's not right. Your choices update to match whether it's a Watcher or a Deterrent.

The connection line

The small grey line under each gadget says how it's hooked up — which camera port, USB slot, or network address. It's just there for troubleshooting; you don't need to change it. Hover it to see the raw technical details.

Adding one by hand

If a gadget didn't turn up in the scan — it's offline, brand new, or you just want to try the app without real hardware — add it here by typing a name and picking what it is.

Alarm roles

A trigger starts an alarm (a radar, motion sensor, or a camera you want to act as a tripwire). A confirmer adds camera evidence to corroborate one. A gadget can be both, one, or neither.

Motion trigger

Instead of treating every photo as an alarm, watch this camera for movement and only raise an alarm when something actually moves. The watching happens right on your hub — only a confirmed sighting is sent up.

Monitor zone

Drag a box over just the area you want watched (e.g. the garden bed) — movement elsewhere is ignored. When using a keyboard, press Enter or Space to watch the whole frame.
