Help & setup

Everything you need to get your garden watched over — how protection works, how to set up the Garden Node, and what the dashboard is telling you.

How protection works

Garden Protector keeps an eye on your garden and, when an unwanted visitor turns up, gives them a harmless squirt of water to send them on their way. You're always in charge with three simple modes:

Off
Taking a break — nothing is watched and nothing will spray. Handy while you're out pottering in the garden yourself.
Monitor
Watches and keeps a photo diary of who visits, but never sprays. A gentle "log only" mode for when you just want to see what's about.
Active
The full deal — watches the garden and gives a confirmed critter a quick squirt to shoo it off.
Spraying & the Stop button
When a squirt is happening, the dashboard shows Spraying and a Stop this spray button appears. Tapped it by mistake, or it's just next door's cat you don't mind? Stop the squirt — protection stays on and is ready for the next visitor. (When it's raining, sprays pause on their own and resume once it's dry.)

Set up your Garden Node

The Garden Node is the little weatherproof box that lives out in the garden and does the watching. Inside it's a tiny low-power brain (an ESP32-C3) wired to a motion radar that spots visitors, a relay that switches the water-spray on and off, and its own battery (topped up by a small solar panel) so it runs all day without a cable to the house. Fit a weather sensor too and it'll add temperature, humidity and rain to your dashboard.

What you'll need:

  • The Garden Node box (brain + motion radar)
  • Your spray gadget — a small pump and nozzle, plumbed to a water jug
  • Power for the garden: a 12 V battery and a small solar panel
  • A computer with a USB-C cable for the one-time setup
  • Optional weather extras: a temperature/humidity sensor and a rain gauge

Getting it going:

  1. Load the software. Plug the node into your computer with the USB-C cable and pop the Garden Protector software onto it — a one-time step that teaches the box its job.
  2. Power it up. Connect the battery (and solar panel) and place the box out in the garden where it can see the beds you want to protect, with the spray pointed the right way.
  3. Pair it with your garden. Switch protection to Active from the dashboard. Within a minute the box says hello and the Garden Node tile turns online — you're protected. If it stays offline, double-check it has power and is within Wi-Fi range of the house.
Good to know: if the node ever loses power or drops off, the spray simply stays switched off — it always fails safe, so a flat battery never means a soggy garden.

What the dashboard tiles mean

The dashboard's health tiles show what your Garden Node is reporting. Until a node has paired, you'll see NOT SET UP (or OFFLINE if it's gone quiet) — tap that tile any time to jump back to this guide.

Garden Node — is the garden box awake and talking to the house? Online means you're protected; offline or "not set up" means it isn't reporting yet.
Battery — how much charge the garden box has left. The solar panel tops it back up during the day.
Temperature — how warm it is out in the garden right now (needs the optional weather sensor).
Humidity — how damp the air is (also from the weather sensor).
Rain — whether it's raining. When it is, sprays pause automatically — critters shelter in the rain, so there's nothing to shoo — and pick back up once it's dry.

A dash (—) on a tile just means that reading hasn't come in yet, usually because the matching sensor isn't fitted.
